Unfortunately, the current version of the EDP code and algorithm does not allow us to use it for such an extensive simulation studies since for the standard simulation, it would take roughly 27.5 hours, while for ZIC-BCF, it takes roughly 30 minutes (i.e., the proposed model is approximately 55 times more efficient).

Consequently, reproducing the EDP+ZI benchmark across the full design, three data-generating processes, the sample-size sensitivity grid (N ∈ {100, 250, 500, 1000}), and the four zero-inflation levels, each at 100 Monte-Carlo replications with 1,000 burn-in and 1,000 retained MCMC iterations, would require on the order of 1,080 hours (≈ 45 days) of uninterrupted computation on a single machine. This estimate anchors on a measured 36 minutes for two simulation for the standard N = 500 configuration and the sampler's cubic O(N³) per-sweep cost, which alone makes the N = 1000 cell cost ~220 h. Because this is infeasible on the freely available compute used for the rest of the study (whose sessions are capped at ~12 h and subject to disconnection), the EDP+ZI model was implemented and verified for correctness but unfortunately excluded from the empirical comparison.
